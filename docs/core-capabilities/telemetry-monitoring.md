# Telemetry & Monitoring

Connect's telemetry layer transforms raw device data into actionable insight. By continuously collecting signals from sensors, gateways, and edge controllers, the platform helps organisations reduce waste, optimise energy usage, and maintain healthier, more resilient estates.

Every widget on this dashboard is designed to surface the most important operational indicators at a glance. Whether it's identifying offline hardware, spotting communication failures, or tracking estate-wide availability, Connect gives users the visibility they need to act early and act confidently.

## Widget Breakdown

### Stores Online

**What it shows:**  
The total number of stores currently connected and reporting telemetry.

**Why it matters:**  
A high online count indicates healthy estate connectivity. Drops in this number can signal network issues, power failures, or site-level outages that require investigation.

### Commander Box Offline

**What it shows:**  
The number of Commander units that are currently offline or not responding.

**Why it matters:**  
Commander devices act as the local control and orchestration layer. Offline units can lead to gaps in automation, missed sensor readings, and reduced operational efficiency. Early detection helps prevent energy waste and ensures local systems continue to run safely.

### Air Connect Gateway Errors

**What it shows:**  
The total number of Air Connect gateways reporting communication or hardware errors.

**Why it matters:**  
Gateways are the bridge between sensors and the cloud. Errors here can cause blind spots in telemetry, delayed alerts, or inaccurate environmental readings. Monitoring this widget helps teams quickly isolate failing gateways and restore full visibility.
